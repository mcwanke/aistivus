## 2. Pre-Draft Checklist (AI runs this before every letter)

Before writing a single word of the letter, the AI must:

1. **Research the company.** Fetch the company website, About, Mission,
   Culture, and Values pages. Note any language or themes worth mirroring.
2. **Review the JD.** Identify the role archetype, key themes, and
   any signals about company culture and communication style.
3. **Check for known gaps.** Cross-reference your background
   (jobsearch.md Section 3) against the JD. Flag any gaps that warrant
   the optional gap block.
4. **Propose the following and ask for confirmation before drafting:**
   - **Salutation:** suggest one option with brief rationale
   - **Tone descriptor:** one word (e.g. warm, sharp, playful, formal,
     earnest) with brief rationale based on company culture read
   - **Hook suggestion:** 2-3 sentences describing the opening angle
     proposed, grounded in company research
   - **Gap acknowledgment:** flag if the optional gap block is recommended
     and why
5. **Before delivering:** verify Block B's company-specific closing
   line has been updated for this company. It is the most common
   copy-paste error across reviewed letters.
6. **Wait for confirmation, adjustment, or override.**
   Do not draft the letter until confirmed.

## 3. Hook Guidance

Every cover letter opens with a hook that is unique to this company
and this application. There are no templates here — only principles.

### What makes a strong hook
- **Personal experience with the product or mission.** Real and specific.
  "I use this product. That is not something I can say about every role."
- **A moment of genuine recognition.** Discovering why the company's
  core problem is worth solving — in your own words.
- **A playful reframe of the role.** When your leadership philosophy
  maps directly onto the role, let that be the hook.
- **An honest personal stake.** Vulnerability used with purpose and
  relevance to the company's mission.
- **A direct challenge to convention.** Unexpected voice-forward opener
  that could not appear in any other letter.

### What makes a weak hook
- Generic excitement ("I am thrilled to apply...")
- Mission statement paraphrasing without personal connection
- Leading with credentials before establishing connection
- Anything that could appear in any cover letter for any company

### Hook seed process
The AI proposes a hook based on company research. You can:
- Accept it and proceed
- Provide a different seed (a word, story, angle, or phrase)
- Ask for 2-3 alternative hook options

## 6. Generation Instructions

### Required inputs (AI asks for these if not provided)
- Company name and role title
- Job description (full text)
- Company website URL (AI will fetch if not provided)
- Hook seed (optional — AI proposes if not provided)
- Tone descriptor (optional — AI proposes if not provided)
- Any personal notes or scratchpad context about this company

### Workflow
1. Fetch and read the company website and culture/mission pages
2. Run the Pre-Draft Checklist (Section 2)
3. Propose salutation, tone, and hook — wait for confirmation
4. Draft the letter using confirmed inputs + jobsearch.md as fact source
5. Apply all Always/Never rules from Section 1 of this file
   and jobsearch.md tailoring rules
6. Check word count — must be 450–600 words. Tighten if over.
7. Deliver the letter. Do not offer resume generation in the same response.

### Block adaptation guidance
Blocks in Section 4 are starting points. The AI should:
- Preserve the core idea and factual claims of each block
- Freely rewrite wording to match the confirmed tone and company context
- Ensure the full letter reads as one cohesive voice — not stitched paragraphs
- Flag if any factual claim in an adapted block could be challenged in interview

### File maintenance
When a new letter produces a block variation that is notably stronger,
the AI should note it at the end of the session:
> "Block [X] had a strong variation in this letter worth archiving.
>  Want me to update jobsearch_cover.md?"

Confirm before any update is written.
